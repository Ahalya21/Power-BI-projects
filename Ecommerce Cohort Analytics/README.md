E-Commerce Customer Cohort Analytics

Project Overview

The E-Commerce Customer Cohort Analytics project analyzes customer behavior over time using cohort analysis techniques. The objective is to understand customer retention, purchasing patterns, lifetime value, and silent churn behavior to help businesses make data-driven marketing and retention decisions.
This project combines Python (data processing & modeling) and Power BI (dashboard visualization) to generate actionable insights.


Business Objectives

Analyze customer retention month over month
Identify high-value and low-retention cohorts
Detect  churn customers
Forecast future revenue using time series models
Provide interactive dashboards for business stakeholders


Dataset Description

The dataset contains e-commerce transactional data with fields such as:
Customer ID
Order ID
Order Date
Revenue
Quantity
Product Category
Customer Tenure
Purchase Frequency



Key Analysis Performed

1. Cohort Analysis
Customers grouped by First Purchase Month
Monthly retention matrix created
Cohort-wise revenue and customer count analysis

2.  Customer Segmentation
Repeat vs One-time buyers
High-value customers
At-risk customers

3.  Churn Detection
Identified customers inactive for defined time period
Created churn risk scoring logic
Categorized customers into:
Low Risk
Medium Risk
High Risk

4.  Revenue Forecasting
Time series forecasting using SARIMAX model
Generated:
       Forecast Revenue
       Upper Confidence Interval
       Lower Confidence Interval

Sample Insights

Certain cohorts show strong 3-month retention but drop sharply afterward.
20% of customers contribute to nearly 60% of revenue.
Silent churn customers significantly impact long-term revenue.
Forecast model predicts steady growth with seasonal fluctuations.
